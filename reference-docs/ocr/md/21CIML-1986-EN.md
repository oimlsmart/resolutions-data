# OPENING ADDRESS by Mr. K. BIRKELAND President of the International Committee of Legal Metrology

</div>

Ladies and Gentlemen, Dear Colleagues,

After the commemorative ceremony （*）of 30 years of OIML which allowed us to realise how hard and intelligently the creators of OIML have toiled, it is now time for us to start our work with, I hope, a will to do as well as our predecessors so that in 20 or 30 years time our successors may have the same praise for us.

First of all my thanks to you all for keeping our rendezvous which I arranged for about 18 months after our meeting in Helsinki.

In 18 months many changes occured in the composition of our Committee.

Firstly, I am happy to greet the representative of the People's Republic of China, our 50th Member State, which joined us in April 1985 and participates for the first time officially in our work. Welcome Mr. SONG, you are already well known to many of us having participated in our meeting in Helsinki, in October 1984. Welcome also to the other Members of your Delegation.

As far as the other Member States are concerned the changes are as follows:

- for the German Democratic Republic Mr. LIERS whose retirement was announced in Helsinki has been replaced by Mr. HASCHE,

- for Australia it is Mr. HARVEY who replaces Mr. PETRY who had to retire for health reasons,

- for Brazil Mr. VEADO has replaced Mr. dos SANTOS,

- — for Cameroon Mr. DIFFO has replaced Mr. NDOUGOU,

- for Republic of Korea Mr. CHANG has replaced Mr. KIM,

- for Democratic People's Republic of Korea Mr. KIM HI-SANG has replaced Mr. KIM SUNG-HWAN,

- for Greece Mr. DESIS has replaced Madam GITZENI,

- for Hungary Mr. BELEDI has replaced Mr. GACSI,

- for Indonesia Mr. PUTERA who has participated in many meetings of the Committee and Conferences was nominated to replace Mr. HAROEN,

- for Ireland Mr. FANNING has replaced Mr. McGRATH,

- for Netherlands Mr. NIEUWLAND, well known by all our Members, has replaced Mr. BIJLOO,

- for United-Kingdom Mr. CLAPHAM, who has left the National Physical Laboratory to direct the National Weights and Measures Laboratory, has replaced Mr. SOUCH,

- for Switzerland, Mr. KOCH has replaced Mr. PERLSTAIN who as we were informed in Helsinki has taken his retirement,

- for Tanzania Mr. TUKAI who participated in our meeting in Helsinki was nominated to replace Mr. KABALO, and finaly,

- for Yugoslavia Mr. MEZEK has replaced Mr. BEVK.

One State which had not yet designated its Member of the Committee is represented here; it is Pakistan whose delegate is Mr. ASAD HASAN.

To all these new Members, or rather all those who are present I express my most warm welcome.

This 21st Committee meeting, placed between the 7th and 8th Conferences is of particular importance especially taking into account the evolution and speedy diversification of our activities, the important problems which we have to resolve in order to organize our work, the new questions which face a certain number of International Institutions, us amongst them.

There is a great number of points on our Agenda and this indicates that in less than three days we have to review almost all the problems concerning OIML. In many cases we have to make certain that the decisions of the Conference have been correctly implemented.

Three points, in particular, require our attention.

Firstly, the planning of work.

Several reasons have led us to attempt to improve the planning of the work of our Secretariats : increased number of Reporting Secretariats, increased interaction between the OIML Secretariats themselves and also with the working groups of other Institutions, necessity to define the priorities because our resources do not permit us to deal with all the problems at the same time, necessity to provide a clear picture of our activities for those outside OIML.

The planning techniques themselves do not provide a solution of such problems but they help in finding the most rational solutions. Of course, to achieve this, planning has to be flexible, properly adapted, in brief intelligent ! On the other hand, planning by itself should not create useless work ; planning is not an end in itself, it is a useful tool. I think this is the way planning, of which certain conclusions will be examined by us today, was regarded by the Presidential Council as well as by all the Pilot Secretariats.

The second subject which I would like to mention on this occasion is certification.

Two years ago we decided to interrupt our attempt of setting up an OIML Certification System, because on the strength of available evidence it seemed that such a system could not be set up immediately. However, this break is only temporary and we must be ready to take up our studies again as soon as the right moment arrives in the future, perhaps even the near future.

Whilst we are waiting, punctual actions should allow us to prepare this future. In this sense the 21st meeting assumes a special importance because a certain number of these actions are the subject of decisions which you have to take. Our present objective is, above all, to pave the way which would lead to an OIML Certification System, whatever its form. For this purpose also, we have to continue our co-operation with all the other International Institutions, working in the field of certification.

The third subject I would mention is not a point of the Agenda. It is however of extreme importance and in addition it allows me to state with satisfaction that justifiably any confidence in the technical capabilities of OIML is not misplaced.

In fact, even if it is not yet resolved in every detail, the problem of the electronic measuring instruments was the subject of studies and of really decisive conclusions.

The basic International Document is being printed; right now, draft Recommendations are being elaborated and one of those, the one on electronic weighing, is being submitted to our vote.

The co-operation with IEC is proceeding well and other Institutions follow the footsteps of OIML and are ready to utilise the results of our work.

May I congratulate all those who contributed to this success; in particular, the experts of SP2-Sr6 and SP7-Sr2, the Netherlands and the USA, helped by their Danish colleagues, the Bureau and all Members of their International Working Groups were, after several years of intensive work in almost annual meetings, able to resolve almost all the problems put to them, and meet the challenge they were facing.

Ladies, Gentlemen, Dear Colleagues,

Nothing is perfect in our world and certainly there remains a lot to be done to improve OIML, to accelerate its work and to find new resources.

Nevertheless, a lot of progress has been made during the last years, extending the growth which our Organization has experienced since its creation.

This success is in a great measure your doing since it is you as Members of the Committee who are able to convince your Authorities, your collaborators, and also the other national bodies concerned with metrology that it is necessary to work with OIML because our Organization could, within its competence and due to its efficiency, help them to resolve the standardization problems which they are facing.

We shall now begin our work but before I ask the Director of the Bureau to start with the roll-call of the participants, perhaps the representative of our new Member-State would wish to say a few words?

Mr. SONG the floor is yours.

## 3. FINANCIAL SITUATION

## 3.1. Approval of the accountant report for 1984

No comment was received on this report and the report is adopted by the Committee, who instructs its President and the Director of the Bureau to submit it to the 8th Conference.

## 3.2. Examination of the accountant report for 1985

Mr. ATHANE presents this report which was sent to the Committee Members too late to be examined by the financial Authorities of the Member States and to be adopted by the Committee in session.

After this presentation, the Committee instructs its President and the Director of the Bureau to submit the report for adoption during its 22nd meeting.

## 3.3. Examination of the financial estimates for 1986

Mr. ATHANE presents these estimates which were already submitted to the Committee Members by correspondence. It appears that the actual expenses for most of the budgetary lines, will be equal to or even less than the amounts authorized by the 7th Conference if recalculated to take into account the reduced inflation in France. For certain budgetary lines corresponding to the expenses which are not evenly distributed over the four years of the financial period, the comparison is not so easy and certain of those which exceed the authorization are explained. It is evident however than the receipts are noticeably greater than estimated by the Conference.

Mr. CLAPHAM is disturbed by the considerable overruns of the printing costs for the years 1985 and 1986. He understands that these expenses would be greater especially for the year or two years which follow a meeting of the Conference, but he considers that in any case, the credits voted by the Conference for this purpose will be greatly exceeded.

Mr. ATHANE recognizes that the decisions of the Conference were taken on the basis of under-estimating the cost of printing. In fact :

- on the one hand the Bureau has considered appropriate to print documents which up to now were produced in the form of photocopies (for example : the English language version of the report on the Conference),

- on the other hand the work of the Secretariats has increased and, in addition to the International Recommendations sanctioned by the Conference, a great number of International Documents will be printed,

- finally, the Bureau has undertaken to publish or to republish certain documents of general character (Working Method, List of Secretariats, Guide...).

Mr. ATHANE concludes that in his opinion the OIML publications are the image of the Organization and that the greatest care should be taken in this respect even if it is expensive. He proposes that the 8th Conference should carefully examine this question.

Mr. CLAPHAM declares himself satisfied with the explanations of the Bureau.

In reply to questions by Mr. EDGERLY, Mr. ATHANE gives the following indications :

- a word processor would be bought by the Bureau, end of 1986 or beginning of 1987,

- the amounts of the receipts originating from the sale of publications appears on the annual accountant reports and represent about 1 % of the OIML budget.

In reply to a question from Mr. MUHE, it is explained that the recent European monetary rearrangement will have no effect on the contributions of the Member States expressed in French-Francs, and will have very little effect on the OIML expenses.

## 4. BUREAU STAFF

## 4.1. Examination of posts for which contracts expire in the near future

On the proposal of Mr. BIRKELAND, the Committee accepts the request of the Hungarian Authorities and of Mr. Petik to extend his contract for a period of two years starting from the end of his current contract.

## 4.2. BIML staff regulations - General information and examination of individual cases

Mr. ATHANE recalls that the BIML staff regulations were adopted by the Committee in 1972 (replacing the former regulations) and were then modified several times. The Bureau has the intention to update and to publish these regulations.

In addition, the Committee accepts two proposals made by the Director of the Bureau concerning the situation of some of the staff :

the work of the Administrator having greatly increased since the creation of BIML, with in particular, wider financial responsibility and accounting work, the salary scale of this Officer is modified,

- two of the staff, the Principal secretary and the Documentation clerk, having joined the Bureau very young, have now reached the last steps of the salary scales; the Director of the Bureau is authorized to extend their salary scales by one or two additional increments (this decision applying personally to the two staff members in question and not to the posts they occupy).

On a proposition by Mr. EDGERLY, supported by Mr. ISSAEV, Mr. BIRKELAND decides that the Presidential Council should examine the total of the salaries of the personnel of the Bureau comparing them with the salaries of other Organizations (in particular BIPM) and if necessary prepare proposals for the 8th Conference.

## 4.3. OIML retirement plan

Mr. ATHANE gives reasons for which the Committee is requested to modify two articles of the rules of OIML retirement plan, so that the interests produced by the moneys of the retirement fund be transferred into the general OIML budget, and not into the retirement fund :

the present system, in order to be managed conveniently, would require a special bank account for the retirement fund, that is an additional OIML account, OIML using already 7 different accounts,

the retirement fund would then include a part which could not be calculated in advance because of varying interest rates,

in order to improve the income from the available moneys it would be preferable to be able to invest larger sums whilst on its own the retirement fund could produce only limited interests,

the fact of transferring the interests either into the retirement fund on the general OIML budget would not change the overall credit requirements.

The Italian delegation reads the following declaration, prepared by its Government:

The financial Italian Authorities are expressing a reservation concerning the proposition to modify article 6 of the rules of the retirement plan proposing to transfer into the general OIML budget the interests accrued from the investments of the retirement fund, the management of which should remain autonomous. In fact, these interests must be used to increase the resources of the fund in order to provide for the cost of the retirement benefits. On the contrary, if these interests are accounted for in the budget as income of the Organization, the Member States would have to cover all the charges not covered by the retirement fund whilst it is known that they already cover 2/3 of the fund, given that 1/3 is covered by the personnel and in consequence concerning the financial estimates for 1986 which we have just examined, the Italian delegation expresses reservations, also in respect of the receipts relating to the interests on investments which should not include, for the reasons already given, the interests on the retirement fund ].

Mr. KOCH expresses reservation in relation to the legal aspect of the problem in Switzerland, the sums put aside in a retirement fund in order to be paid eventually as retirement pensions as well as the interests produced by these sums belong to the employees, even if it is being managed by the employer. Mr. KOCH follows on stating that it is quite possible to invest the moneys of the retirement fund with other moneys provided that one calculates the part of interests which have to revert to the retirement fund. Mr. ATHANE considers that this solution would cause practical difficulties.

Madam HENRION and Mr. OHLON express the same reservations as their Swiss colleague.

Mr. HASCHE does not see any objection to the proposal of the Director of the Bureau provided that it would not result in any modification of the contributions of the Member States.

In answer to questions from Messrs NIEUWLAND and KOCH, respectively, Mr. ATHANE indicates that :

at present, he is not aware of any details of the operation of the retirement plan of BIPM,

- in the case of the dissolution of OIML (or serious financial problems), the question of retirement pensions would have to be examined at the same time as other financial questions.

In reply to Mr. HARVEY, it is confirmed that OIML pays no taxes on the interests produced by investments, it being understood that individual retired officers receiving a pension would have to fulfil their obligations vis-à-vis the tax regulations of their countries.

After these comments several Committee Members, among them Messrs ISSAEV and PETERSEN declare that the proposition of the Bureau should be accepted.

Mr. BIRKELAND then, requests the Committee to take a decision by a nominal vote of which the results are as follows:

YES: F.R. of Germany, German D.R., Australia, Austria, Brazil, Bulgaria, Cameroon Canada, P.R. of China, D.P.R. of Korea, Cuba, Denmark, Egypt, Spain United States of America, Finland, France, Hungary, Indonesia, Ireland Japan, Kenya, Monaco, Norway, Pakistan, Netherlands, Poland, United Kingdom, Tanzania, Czechoslovakia, Tunisia, USSR, Yugoslavia.

NO : Belgium, Italy, Sweden, Switzerland.

The proposal of the Bureau is thus accepted.

## 6. PLANNING OF WORK

## 6.1. EXAMINATION OF THE 1985-1988 DRAFT WORK PROGRAMMES

Mr. BIRKELAND recalls the reasons for which the Conference decided to introduce a planning procedure in the working method of the OIML Secretariats. He summarizes the action undertaken for this purpose by the Committee, the Presidential Council and the various Pilot and Reporting Secretariats resulting in the presentation of the draft work programmes to the Committee which in certain cases are accompanied by comments of Member States or the Bureau. He then requests the Committee Members representing the countries responsible for Pilot Secretariats to present the work programmes they are concerned with. As pointed out by Mr. BIRKELAND, these brief presentations are not intended to start a discussion, except in special cases, or when there are basic objections against the draft, or in case of proposals to create or to suppress Secretariats. The detailed comments shall be examined by the experts of the Pilot and Reporting Secretariats concerned who are free to introduce any necessary improvements or modifications into the work programmes.

(BIML note : these presentations which in most cases reproduce the contents of the work programmes are not presented in this report. Only the decisions and discussions relating to certain Secretariats are given below).

SP 12-Sr 1 « Terminology (temperature) »

SP 13-Sr 2 « Wattmeters »

SP 13-Sr 7 « Terminology (electricity) »

being understood that these creations become final only if the conditions laid down in the « Working method of the OIML Secretariats » are complied with. In addition, the Bureau is instructed to carry out inquiries concerning the creation of international working groups of these Secretariats.

The Committee instructs the Presidential Council to examine the situation of the following Secretariats :

- SP1 « Terminology » as well as the Reporting-Secretariats dealing with the terminology within other Pilot-Secretariats,

- — SP 14 « Acoustics and vibration »,

- SP16 « Ionizing radiation » and SP17-Sr3 « Measurement of radionuclide pollution 》，

— SP 27-Sr 2 « Classification of reference materials »

as well as the possible creation of new Secretariats which should deal with « uncertainties of measurement » and « recalibration intervals » (within SP21 and SP23 respectively).

Finally, the Committee instructs the Bureau to carry out an inquiry concerning the possible creation of new Secretariats which should deal with mass meters (within SP 5D) and luxmeters (within a Pilot-Secretariat yet to be defined).

The following Secretariats were the subject of discussions:

## 7.3. Different types of OIML publications

Mr. EDGERLY indicates that this question was broached by the Presidential Council and that the Council instructed him to present a study on this subject to the Committee. He presents a summary of this study with the following conclusions:

a) there is no need to change the status and aims of International Recommendations,

b) the definition of International Documents given in the « Working method of OIML Secretariats » is sufficiently wide to permit publication of all types of manuals, guides, etc. which the Committee considers suitable as having the character of International Documents. It is however recommended that the International Documents continue to be approved by the Committee.

c) there is an increasing demand for a third type of publications which would cover the needs such as the consolidation of Recommendations and Documents, the reports of seminars, various articles, etc. These special publications could be published by decision of the Bureau. There remains however one difficulty to solve ; in the case of a compilation of Recommendations, should the original Recommendations be superseded, or should the Recommendations first be revised if a modification is required ? In the opinion of Mr. EDGERLY the consolidation should not modify the technical contents of the original Recommendations which remain the responsibility of the originating Reporting Secretariats, especially in case of revision or updating.

Mr. THULIN recalls that for many years now the Bureau has been drafting and publishing brochures, without considering it necessary neither to give them a name nor to number them, in order to avoid confusion with the official OIML publications (RI's and DI's). There was in fact already on some occasions, confusion between the numbering of the RI's and DI's, and a third category of publications with its own numbering would increase the risk of confusion.

Mr. EDGERLY considers that on the contrary, the numbering of the new category of publications would permit easy identification and would facilitate references to these publications.

Mr. KOCH considers that it would be possible to date the special publications instead of numbering them.

Mr. PETERSEN touches upon a particular point of the proposals of Mr. EDGERLY, the consolidations of the existing Recommendations. He considers that it is a very interesting possibility, the more so that one could use this consolidation in order to update the technical requirements which were overtaken by modern technology. However, one question arises: should the original Recommendations remain as drafted (in that case the compilation is a simple grouping together), or should they be replaced by new Recommendations which are consolidated ? In general, Denmark would prefer the second solution.

Mr. THULIN indicates that there is a difficulty of translation into French of the English « consolidated ]. The action could consist of a sort of selection and a summary of information contained in the Recommendations, which may be dangerous. On the other hand one could proceed as in ISO by publishing « Handbooks » which are simply collections of standards on the same subject published as handbooks in a smaller size but with no modification or interpretation of the text of these standards ; this is something very useful.

Mr. CLAPHAM agrees with the majority of the proposals of Mr. EDGERLY with the exception of those concerning the publication of compilations of Recommendations without the approval of at least the Committee ; this would risk diminishing the status of those compilations.

Mr. BERTRAN considers that on the contrary there would be no danger of this happening and he quotes as example the AFNOR publications. He requests however that attention should be given to the designation of this third category of publications and especially to the abbreviations to be used since in English "Special publication " would be rendered as SP, a possible confusion with the Pilot Secretariats.

Mr. THULIN recalls that the abbreviations and initials used in OIML should be those as used in the French language (RI and not IR, DI and not ID, etc).

Mr. EDGERLY expresses first of all his agreement with the comment made by Mr. BERTRAN. Concerning the interventions from Messrs CLAPHAM and PETER-SEN, he explains that in his opinion the compilations should change nothing of the original Recommendations, except for editorial modifications. As example, he quotes what was done by Australia for the OIML Recommendations on weighing which were rearranged as a manual giving the manufacturers the metrological, technical and administrative requirements. At present, a manufacturer of weighing instruments has to examine half a dozen of separate Recommendations, which means that he is expected to know that these Recommendations exist, which may not always be the case. The compilations should therefore change nothing of the original Recommendations, which rest under the responsibility of the originating Reporting Secretariats and must remain in existence as individual Recommendations.

Mr. BIRKELAND expresses some apprehension concerning the extend of the modifications which could be introduced in respect of the existing Recommendations by means of the compilation process. In particular, he has some hesitation on the advisability to authorize a Reporting Secretariat or the Bureau to prepare publications presenting modifications, even editorial ones, in respect of the Recommendations already sanctioned by the Conference.

Mr. HARVEY considers that the proposal from Mr. EDGERLY may lead to a great number of modifications of the original texts, even if these modifications are only editorial. Since Mr. EDGERLY has mentioned the Design manuals

prepared by Australia he would like to point out that these manuals only regroup several OIML Recommendations on the same subject and reproduce very exactly the requirements.

Mr. IIZUKA considers that the compilation work should be left to the national authorities: they should first translate the OIML Recommendations into the national language and then elaborate the compilations of these translations.

Mr. ISSAEV supports the point of view expressed by the Japanese delegate.

In conclusion of these discussions, Mr. BIRKELAND decides that the Presidential Council should study in detail the whole of the problem and then propose to the Committee to take decisions on the following basis:

it should be officially recognized that the Bureau, on its own or with the participation of a Secretariat or another working group may prepare and decide to publish publications which are neither International Recommendations nor International Documents,

the designation of these publications, the manner of their indexing, etc. must be examined,

the problem of consolidation of Recommendations must be studied separately, since there may not fit into any of the above mentioned categories of publications; in fact we may be dealing with two types of compilations :

a) several Recommendations concerning the same subject may be assembled without any modifications and the set is published in an appropriate form : this could be a decision of the Bureau, the original Recommendations remaining in force,

b) on the basis of several Recommendations, a unique text is elaborated intended to replace the original Recommendations (updated if necessary) ; this procedure should be under the control of the Committee or even of the Conference, since the original Recommendations would disappear.

In addition, the compilation could in certain cases be replaced by an « analytical summary » giving for a set of Recommendations dealing with the same subject the location of individual requirements; these summaries could be prepared by the originating Secretariat and be published by the Bureau.

Mr. BIRKELAND then broaches the particular problem of the OIML vocabularies. In the name of the Polish delegation, Mr. PESTRAKIEWICZ gives a summary of the SP1 proposal :

- to examine the advisability of creating a special designation and numbering system for the different OIML vocabularies, and

— to set up Reporting Secretariats « Terminology » in all the fields of OIML work.

Mr. BIRKELAND decides that this question should be examined by the Presidential Council, in liaison with that of the different categories of publications.

The Polish delegation expresses agreement but insists on the urgency of this study.

## 13. OIML CERTIFICATION

## 13.1. Various activities

Mr. PETIK recalls that the question of mutual recognition of metrological controls started to be studied about 10 years ago. An ad hoc group was instructed to study the methods of operation of an OIML certification system. At the end of the work of this ad hoc group the Committee noted that for economic and political reasons beyond its control such a system could not be set up at present. Waiting for this to become possible, SP2-Sr4 under the responsibility of the Bureau was instructed to study the possibility of punctual actions which could favor the eventual setting up of an OIML certification system.

Four activities were defined and are proposed for study by the Committee :

a) compilation, classification and dissemination of information concerning bi- and multilateral agreements on the recognition of test results, pattern approvals and verifications,

b) possibility of a National Service of Metrology to take note of the reports on pattern testing by Services of other countries,

c) reference to OIML requirements in the national decisions of pattern approval,

d) declarations of conformity for export.

Concerning the activities a), b) and d), the Committee adopts the following resolutions:

a) The International Committee of Legal Metrology,

HAVING TAKEN NOTE of the report of SP 2-Sr 4,

- a distribution of this information,

HAVING TAKEN NOTE of the report of SP 2-Sr 4,

## 13.3. Directives for future activities

Mr. ATHANE recalls that one of the actions proposed by SP 2-Sr4 (reference to OIML in the national pattern approval decisions) was not accepted by the Committee and it may therefore be appropriate to reflect on this subject. Furthermore, it may be appropriate to examine the possibility of a common action with IEC and ISO whenever there exist simultaneous OIML RI's/DI's and ISO/IEC Standards for the same category of instruments. BIPM could also be associated whenever certification is based on measurement standards traceable to those of BIPM.

Mr. ISSAEV supports this last idea and indicates that CMEA is in the process of examining the possibility to co-operate with other Institutions on the bases of some already existing CMEA documents.

Mr. OHLON also supports this proposal. With reference to this subject he recalls the procedure of a closer co-operation between the European Community and the European Free Trade Association intended to eliminate technical barriers to trade and to create a《West European Common Market 》。In this connection, he describes the action of the Swedish Administration in order to harmonize the national regulations with those of the EEC and to participate in the new approach in the matter of technical regulations which would call on CEN and CENELEC standards. He is pleased about the information given by the Bureau on the closer co-operation OIML-CEN-CENELEC (see point 10 above) but he recommends that in adition OIML should follow very closely the certification activities within Western Europe and to participate in those as far as possible.

Mr. CLAPHAM expresses serious reservations with reference to the second proposal of Mr. ATHANE. In fact, the Members of the Committee are concerned with legal metrology and measuring instruments within the field of legal metrology and, in this connection, one should recognize that the OIML Recommendations are effectively developed with reference to their application in legal metrology which is not necessarily the case for the ISO and IEC standards. The OIML certification system should exclusively be based on OIML RI's and DI's. Mr. CLAPHAM is even more opposed to any association of BIPM in this action. It seems to him impossible for BIPM to deliver certificates for the traceability of working standards to their own standards. This is a matter which concerns exclusively the national laboratories or the accredited calibration centers.

Messrs. EDGERLY and NIEUWLAND fully support the reservations of Mr. CLAPHAM and request that no action should be undertaken before the Committee had time to study in detail the proposal and had taken a decision on this subject.

Mr. BIRKELAND considers that the Committee is not prepared to hold such a discussion at this moment. However, it seems that the developments in the matter of international certification are considerable and rapid; one should note that SP2-Sr4 has correctly accomplished the tasks which were entrusted to it in 1983 but that since then new developments have occured. At his request, the Committee agrees to the suggestions that the Presidential Council should study these developments and should submit new proposals to the Committee in 1987 or 1988. During this time, SP2-Sr4 should continue to study the problem limiting its activity to follow the tasks which were entrusted to it and without taking any new initiatives.

## PRESIDENCY and VICE-PRESIDENCY of the COMMITTEE

Mr. BIRKELAND recalls that he and Mr. ISSAEV were elected as President and Vice-President respectively, in 1980 and that their mandates of 6 years have now been completed.

He assures his colleagues that he acted as President of the Committee with great interest and pleasure during these six rewarding and profitable years even if on occasion difficulties arose which had to be overcome.

He wishes to express his thanks to his colleagues for the co-operation which they have shown him.

He therefore withdraws, with Mr. ISSAEV, and hands over the presidency of the Committee to the Second Vice-President.

Mr. MUHE first of all points out that a very close and fruitful co-operation always existed between the President and the two Vice-Presidents of the Committee as well as the Director of the Bureau.

He reminds his colleagues that Messrs BIRKELAND and ISSAEV have indicated their willingness to accept a renewal of their mandates if this should be the wish of the Committee.

This proposal received unanimous support of the Members of the Presidential Council which met in Braunschweig in September 1985.

Furthermore no other candidate was proposed and Mr. MUHE therefore requests the Members of the Committee to approve the renewal of the mandate of Mr. BIRKELAND as President and that of Mr. ISSAEV as Vice-President.

The Committee indicates its agreement by acclamation and Messrs BIRKELAND and ISSAEV return to their places at the Presidential table.

Messrs. BIRKELAND and ISSAEV express their warm thanks to the assembly and Mr. BIRKELAND then indicates that the mandate of the second Vice-President Mr. MUHE will become vacant the moment he takes his retirement as Director of the Physikalisch Technische Bundesanstalt, in May 1986.

On the proposal by Mr. BIRKELAND the Committee elects by acclamation Mr. EDGERLY, United States of America, as Second Vice-President.

Mr. EDGERLY thanks his colleagues for this honour and sign of confidence and assures them that he will continue to do his best in furthering the objectives and the missions of OIML.

Mr. BIRKELAND then takes the floor again and following his proposal the assembly unanimously nominates as HONORARY MEMBERS of the INTERNATIONAL COMMITTEE of LEGAL METROLOGY Mr. MUHE, Federal Republic of Germany and Mr. LIERS, German Democratic Republic.

Mr. BIRKELAND thanks his colleagues for the effort and work accomplished which permitted to arrive at a number of decisions of importance for the life of OIML.

He comments, in particular, that the Committee was able to concentrate on a study of essential problems, leaving the less important points to other working groups. He is pleased that every body has shown his understanding of the importance and extent of OIML work. In the name of the Committee he thanks Mr. BERTRAN, CIML Member for France, for the very pleasant reception offered by the French Administration. He also expresses his thanks to all the experts of the Pilot and Reporting Secretariats who have contributed so much to the technical contents of the planning work of the Committee. He concludes with a request to his colleagues on the Committee to return to their respective Countries and to watch over the implementation of the decisions of the Committee and invites them to a rendezvous for the next meeting, in September 1987.
